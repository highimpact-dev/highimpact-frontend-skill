Since 2018

Two People, One
Obsession

Ember & Oak started on a kitchen counter in Southeast Portland. We'd been buying green beans from a guy who knew a guy, roasting them in a cast-iron skillet, and keeping notes in a spiral notebook. The coffee was uneven. Sometimes great, sometimes terrible. We couldn't stop.

Six years later, the skillet became a Probat, the notebook became a lab, and the guy who knew a guy became farmers in Huila, Sidamo, and Antigua we visit every harvest. What hasn't changed: we still roast every batch ourselves, and we still can't stop chasing that perfect profile.

The Craft

Three Steps. No Shortcuts.

Every bag that leaves our roastery has been through the same deliberate process. It takes longer. It costs more. The cup is worth it.

01 — Source

Direct From the Farm

We buy green coffee directly from farmers in Colombia, Ethiopia, and Guatemala. No brokers, no bulk lots. We visit during harvest, cup samples at origin, and pay prices that make direct trade actually mean something.

02 — Roast

12 Pounds at a Time

Our 1958 Probat UG-15 handles 12 pounds per batch. Each origin gets its own roast profile developed over weeks of testing. We roast four days a week. Mondays and Thursdays are for dialing in new lots.

03 — Rest

48 Hours. Minimum.

Freshly roasted coffee needs time to degas. We rest every batch for at least 48 hours before it ships. The difference between good coffee and great coffee is often just patience.
